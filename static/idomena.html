  BASIC INFORMATION
Back to top

You are in Idomeni, Greece. 

Thessaloniki and its prefecture are in Macedonia, Greece. The country across the border is called the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia (FYR Macedonia). 

Important!

  • Movement north into Gevgelija (FYR Macedonia) is now restricted to refugees coming from Syria and Iraq. (NOTE: Those coming from Afghanistan are not allowed to enter FYR Macedonia.)
  • In order to cross the border Syrians and Iraqis entering FYR Macedonia must now present official photographic identification. 
     
  • Keep your travel documents and registration information on you at all times. 
  • STAY TOGETHER! All family members should remain together at all times. It is advised that children have the names of family members and a phone number in their clothing in case they are separated.

In Case of Family Separation

If you have been separated from a family member, contact Arsis, UNHCR or Save the Children in Idomeni. Arsis also has a 24-hour hotline that you may call: +30 697 09 94 787. 

 

For people who have been missing for longer than 24 hours, you can call the Athens Reuniting Family Links (RFL) Number: +30 210 825 9002.

* It is never too late to report a missing person – even years after you were separated. You will get a unique number for your case that you may use anywhere the world. Please leave a permanent way for the Red Cross to reach you.

The Red Cross has a free and confidential hotline in Arabic and Farsi which you may also call at +30 210 514 0440. It is open from 08:00-20:00 Monday-Friday, and can give you information about your stay in Greece or about your onward journey.

  REGISTRATION
Back to top

Please keep your registration papers on you at all times. You must have your Greek registration papers with you to continue your journey to FYR Macedonia (not everyone is allowed to do so -- please see Important Information above for more details) or to remain in Greece.

  LODGING
Back to top

There is currently no lodging available in Idomeni. For lodging, you must either continue your trip across the border to FYR Macedonia (not everyone is allowed to do so -- please see important information above for more details) or go to Athens or Thessaloniki. 

  SERVICES
Back to top

The following services are available at Idomeni: 

  • Medical services and referrals in case of emergency (24/7)
  • Free wifi
  • Limited food assistance
  • Blanket and clothing distributions (based on need and availability) 

WARNING: European winters are very cold. If you have warm clothing for yourself and your families, please make sure to keep it throughout your journey. If you do not have these items, organizations on site will provide you with these if they are available.

  TRANSPORTATION TO ATHENS
Back to top

For those nationalities who are not permitted entry into FYR Macedonia, you will be requested by the police to board a bus back to Athens immediately. These buses are at your own expense, and typically cost between €10-€30 (although the most typical fare is €20). There are currently no set schedules for the buses, and they depart when they fill up. Buses currently depart from just in front of the Idomeni site.

Medical support and referral where necessary is provided 24/7 at Idomeni by MSF, MDM, Praksis and the Red Cross. An ambulance can be called and people will be taken to the local hospital in case of an emergency.
