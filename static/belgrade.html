You are in Belgrade, Serbia.

If you arrive by bus or train, you will likely be right next to Bristol Park. In and around Bristol Park there are a number of services to assist you.

Important!

  • Keep your travel documents and registration information on you at all times.
  • STAY TOGETHER! All family members should remain together at all times. It is advised that children have the names of family members and a phone number in their clothing in case they are separated. Agree upon local meeting points with your family in case of separation.

In Case of Family Separation

If you have been separated from a family member, contact the Red Cross where you are.

  • Serbia Restoring Family Links Hotline: +381 (0) 11 26 23 970

If you are interested in declaring asylum in Serbia:

DECLARING ASYLUM IN SERBIA

  REGISTRATION
Back to top

  • It is currently not possible to register in Belgrade if you wish to transit through Serbia; only people seeking asylum can register in Belgrade. 
  • You may put in a request for asylum by going to the Police Station Department for Foreigners (Uprava za strance):

Savska 35
Tel: +381 (0)11 3618 744.
Operating hours: 08:00-20:00 daily.

  • Here, you will be fingerprinted, photographed and provide any documents you have. You will then be given a paper that expresses your interest in applying for asylum in Serbia. 
  • With this paper you will have 72 hours to formally request asylum at an asylum center. 
     
  • For more information on asylum, please click on the "Declaring Asylum in Serbia" button above. 
  • For more details on legal aid organizations that may assist you in seeking asylum and to confirm that you are eligible to seek asylum, please see the services section below. 

  LODGING
Back to top

Krnjača Camp is located 10 km away from Belgrade. Please note that this is an open camp. In other words, you are free to come and go as you please and will not be detained here.

Free buses bring people from just in front of “Hotel Mr. President” Karadjordjeva 75, Belgrade to Krnjača and back.

  • Buses depart from Belgrade to Krnjača daily at: 11:00 and 18:00
  • Buses return to Belgrade from Krnjača daily at: 12:00 and 19:00
  • However, the bus times sometimes change, so please confirm.

 

  • You are not permitted to sleep in the park. If the police see you sleeping in the park, they may pick you up and bring you to Krnjača where you may spend the night.
  • There are also a number of hostels and hotels in Belgrade at different price points. Please be ready to show your registration papers upon check-in.

InfoPark
The InfoPark Kiosk is located within Bristol Park.
Operating Hours: 09:00 to 20:00 daily
They will be able to provide you with detailed information about registration, accommodation, border closures, transportation etc. They will also be able to assist you with things like buying SIM cards and bus/train tickets.

 

Miksalište:
Miksalište is located a few minutes’ walk from Bristol Park.
Mostarska 5
Tel: +381 (0)11 2626 068
Operating Hours: 10:00 to 16:00 daily

There you will find the following:

  • Water and snacks
  • Toilets
  • Phone charging
  • Showers with hot water
  • Laundry facilities
  • Medical support
  • Family reunification efforts
  • Clothes, blankets, shoe distributions
  • Hygiene packs
  • Child friendly space

 

Info Centre
Info Centre is located a few minutes’ walk from Bristol Park.
Nemanjina 3
Operating Hours: 09:00-21:00 Monday-Friday; 9:00-17:00 Saturday and Sunday

They offer:

  • Legal aid (and information on applying for asylum in Serbia)
  • Psychological and psychosocial support
  • Wifi and computers
  • Phone charging
  • Translation services
  • A mothers’ and babies’ space
  • Clothing and shoes
  • Hygiene packs
    ​

Please keep in mind that winters can be very cold, snowy and wet as you move through Europe. It is strongly recommended that you take advantage of the warm clothing available to you in Belgrade. Please ensure that you have a proper jacket, gloves, hat, scarf and footwear, and bring blankets or sleeping bags with you.

 

  • If you would like to purchase a SIM card for your telephone, please keep in mind that it should not cost more than €2 to do so, and you are not required to present any papers or identification upon purchase.

There is medical care available at Bristol Park between 07:00 and 19:00, and at Miksalište from 10:00-16:00.

People with serious medical conditions will be taken to the clinic or the hospital.

Hospital address:
Urgent Care Centre (Urgentni Centar)
Pasterova 2
Tel: +381 (0) 11 3618 444
Operating hours: Open 24 hours a day

You can get to the hospital by taxi (approximately €20-€30) or by ambulance (dial 194).
